GG Winners Batch 1

Cate Blanchett

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A SUPPORTING ROLE IN A MOTION PICTURE

The Award Now a two-time winner, Blanchett tested her acting chops in a new sort of role-a man-in her portrayal of Bob Dylan in I'm Not There. Her voice and demeanor were well-practiced, believable and, as a result, Globe-worthy.
Signature Style You could call her a conservative risk-taker on the red carpet. Her choices are always unique, not usually in sync with the trend du jour, but always stunning. She tends to veer toward metallics or basic, albeit strong, black.
This Look Giorgio Armani, a favorite designer of the Aussie actress, calls her "serene, confident and elegant." In one of his beautiful creations, the expectant mother fits his description perfectly.

Julie Christie

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award Her role as a wife who begins to suffer from Alzheimer's in Away From Her was anything but unforgettable for the Hollywood Foreign Press, who bestowed 66-year-old Christie with her first win and her fourth nomination.
Signature Style A bit of a reluctant celebrity, Christie has never owned a clothes dryer (she prefers to hang-dry, as it's more environmentally friendly). She has said, "I love to see other people looking beautiful, but I'm not someone who likes dressing up." While her style at the time was very '60s mod, these days it's not as easily defined. Sometimes she dabbles in colorful combinations; sometimes she opts for basic black.
This Look For the New York premiere of Away From Her, Christie offered a little nod to her mod phase with black tights under her green dress. The red accents suggest that Christie still likes to reveal her fiery, fun side.

Keira Knightley

The actress picked up her second Golden Globe nomination for her lead role in Atonement. And while she may not have won the Globe this year, she consistently rises to the top when it comes to dressing for the red carpet. Here Knightley stepped out at the premiere of Atonement in a plunging ballerina dress by Rodarte.

Angelina Jolie

The sexy mother of four-who has been nominated for a Golden Globe, Critics' Choice and SAG award for her leading role in A Mighty Heart-stayed covered up in a trench while on the red carpet at the Critics' Choice Awards but revealed a form-fitting Dolce & Gabbana dress once inside. "Black is me-and it's easy since I don't have time to put that much thought into clothes," says Jolie. Occasionally, the actress breaks from her black streak, as she did in a canary-yellow gown for the premiere of A Mighty Heart in Cannes last year.

Golden Globe Winner - Jodie Foster

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award Call it a hat trick: Foster receives her third Globe (from five nominations) for playing a widow seeking revenge in The Brave One.
Signature Style Her inclination toward neutral colors and simple shapes results in classic choices that suit her private persona.
This Look Known to be one of Giorgio Armani's muses, the 45-year-old icon almost exclusively wears his designs on the red carpet, including this unfussy and lovely floral choice.

Golden Globe Winner - George Clooney

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award He's already won best actor awards from the National Board of Review, the San Francisco Film Critics Circle and the Washington, D.C. Area Film Critics Association. Now he can add a Golden Globe (his second) for his role in Michael Clayton as the title character.
Signature Style Can you think of a worst-dressed moment in recent history for this 46-year-old? Neither can we. Always dapper, never flashy, Clooney in a tux deserves a head-to-toe scan every time.
This Look It almost seems unfair how beautiful an Armani suit looks on the actor, who wore one to the Critics' Choice Awards earlier this month.

Golden Globe Winner - Viggo Mortensen

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award] The actor best known for his roles in Lord of the Rings and A History of Violence can now add Golden Globe winner to his resume. The win celebrates his role as Nikolai Luzhin, a mysterious Russian in the drama Eastern Promises.
Signature Style It's hard to believe he'll be 50 years old this year. Mortensen has fun with casual styles, often adding a red shirt or tie to the mix. (He's even worn a two-piece red suit before!)
This Look For most high-profile events, like here at the opening of the San Sebastian Film Festival, Mortensen opts for subtlety, donning traditional black suits and simple ties.

Daniel Day-Lewis

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award The fifth time's a charm: After five noms, Day-Lewis secures a Globe win for his role as Daniel Plainview in There Will Be Blood.
Signature Style This 50-year-old likes to bring out his playful side on the red carpet, as he's done in the past with a three-piece plaid suit or a pink silk ascot.
This Look He's also a pro at nailing a more traditional look, as he did in this tux at the Palm Springs Film Festival.

Golden Globe Winner - James McAvoy

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award In a surprising, though decisively deserving win, 28-year-old McAvoy has beat the industry's best (George Clooney, Denzel Washington, Daniel Day-Lewis and Viggo Mortensen) for this coveted Globe. A first-time nominee and winner, McAvoy's turn as Robbie Turner in Atonement struck a chord with audiences, as did those piercing blue eyes.
Signature Style When he hits the red carpet, McAvoy tends to be unfussy, with a bit of facial scruff and the top few buttons on his shirt undone. This Scotsman has also been known to show up at an event in traditional Scottish knee-grazing kilt.
This Look If he wears a tie, as he did to this Atonement premiere, it's usually on the skinnier side, in the retro fashion popular with many young stars.

golden globe winner - Denzel Washington

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, DRAMA

The Award Washington is now three-for-three at the Globes. He earned his first in 1990, his second in 2000 and now takes home a third for his portrayal of a heroin kingpin in American Gangster.
Signature Style This 54-year-old's premiere uniform typically includes a button-up shirt, jeans and Adidas sneakers. But boy can he strike a dapper pose when he wants to in a custom-made tux.
This Look He's poised and confident in monochromatic black, matching his wife, Pauletta, perfectly.

Amy Adams

The strawberry-blonde star received her first Golden Globe nomination for bringing a fairy-tale princess to life in Enchanted. In real life, Adams ditches the fanciful dresses for body-hugging numbers from designers like Dolce & Gabbana and Fendi. Here, the actress got glam for the premiere of Charlie Wilson's War in a vintage-inspired Oscar de la Renta shift worn with towering Prada heels.

Golden Globe Winner - Ellen Page

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award The first-time winner played a tart (and pregnant) teenager in the critically acclaimed Juno.
Signature Style 20-year-old Page dresses like an Indie rock version of Audrey Hepburn in classic shifts from cutting-edge designers.
This Look The actress attended the Palm Springs International Film Festival Awards in a gray silk dress with a twist: a raw-edged neckline.

Marion Cotillard

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award Cotillard embodied legendary chanteuse Edith Piaf in La Vie En Rose, winning her a first-time Globe.
Signature Style Cotillard shows off her natural beauty-and natural French chic-in designs from her homeland, including lots of Chanel.
This Look The actress opted for a bias-cut Nina Ricci cocktail dress for the Critics' Choice Awards; she accessorized with a strand of Chopard diamonds.

Golden Globe Winner - Helena Bonham Carter

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award The five-time nominee wins her first Globe with her role as a sexy-and singing-Mrs. Lovett in Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street.
Signature Style The British actress doesn't shy away from a statement: she favors Vivienne Westwood corsets and gowns from decades past.
This Look The new mother celebrated partner Tim Burton's lifetime achievement award at the Venice Film Festival in a sophisticated empire-waist cocktail dress.

Golden Globe Winner - Nikki Blonsky

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTRESS IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award The first-time nominee made her big-screen debut in Hairspray, following a nationwide hunt for the perfect Tracy Turnblad.
Signature Style Skipping the '60s shifts she wore in Hairspray, the 19-year-old plays up her curves in '50s-bombshell numbers set off by peep-toe stilettos.
This Look At a recent Hollywood event, Blonsky emphasized her creamy skin in a black cocktail dress worn with a satin bolero jacket.

golden globe winner - Tom Hanks

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award Hanks adds a fifth Globe to his mantel in honor of his title role in Charlie Wilson's War.
Signature Style Who needs trends? Hanks makes the scene in flawlessly tailored suits in classic shapes.
This Look Sophistication at its finest: Hanks chose a dark suit and a pewter tie for the premiere of Charlie Wilson's War.

golden globe winner - Ryan Gosling

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award First-time nominee Gosling captured the Globe with his sensitive portrayal of a shy Midwesterner-with an inanimate girlfriend-in Lars and the Real Girl.
Signature Style Dapper? You bet. Gosling often chooses perfectly-tailored three-piece suits (worn sans tie) for his red-carpet walks.
This Look The 27-year-old actor smoldered at the N.Y.C. premiere of Lars and the Real Girl in a plaid suit and full beard.

Johnny Depp

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award Depp wins his first Golden Globe (on his eighth nomination!) for his role as crooning killer Sweeney Todd: The Demon Barber of Fleet Street.
Signature Style Zoot suits, funny hats, tinted shades, two-toned shoes-Depp has worn them all. And we wouldn't want it any other way.
This Look The actor donned a vintage-inspired white coat and matching oxfords at an event to honor Tim Burton.

golden globe winner - John C. Reilly

BEST PERFORMANCE BY AN ACTOR IN A MOTION PICTURE, MUSICAL OR COMEDY

The Award The two-time nominee accepted his first Globe for his hilarious role in mock-biopic Walk Hard: The Dewey Cox Story.
Signature Style Reilly is happy to don wild getups for special events: he promoted Walk Hard in mariachi-style suits.
This Look The multi-talented actor smiled for the cameras at the Independent Spirit Awards in a classic dark suit worn with an eye-matching blue button-down.
