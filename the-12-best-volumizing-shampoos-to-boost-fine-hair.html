How do volumizing shampoos work?

Full, bouncy hair doesn’t magically appear: The best volumizing shampoos are specially designed to physically lift hair strands away from the root. Cho mentions that Superzero uses vegan keratin as a weightless way to tame flyaways and leave smooth, shiny hair behind. Since volumizing shampoos are well suited for people with thin hair, you’ll see that many formulas also promote hair strengthening and thickening—and that typically starts with the scalp.

“By removing oil and dead skin cells that make the hair appear limp, fine hair immediately looks fuller,” Fekkai sats. Meanwhile, Cho says ingredients like rosemary and caffeine are often used “to stimulate the scalp for people with thinning hair.”

Who are volumizing shampoos best for?

More times than not, volumizing shampoos are best suited for people with fine or thinning hair. “Using a volumizing shampoo helps lift your hair and give the look of fullness and thickness,” Cho explains. “Volumizing can also help address damaged hair issues and strengthen the strands.”

But while there are volumizing shampoos compatible with an array of hair types and textures, Fekkai recommends erring on the side of caution. “Those with dry hair, curly hair, or coarse hair will find a volumizing shampoo sorely lacking in hydration, nourishment, smoothing, and taming,” he says.

How often should you use a volumizing shampoo?

Once you find a formula you love, feel free to use it as often as you wish. “Volumizing shampoo can be used the same as a regular shampoo; the product provides more bounce and lift,” Cho explains. “[It’s a] key first step in achieving a voluminous look and provides a great base for styling.”

Although many volumizing shampoos feature a light formula that won’t weigh your hair down, Cho says it’s important to thoroughly rinse your hair—especially if you choose to incorporate a conditioner. “[That way,] the hair feels clean, light, [with] bounce to last all day.”
